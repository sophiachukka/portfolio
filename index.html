About

Turning data into decisions that matter

Hi! I'm Sophia Chukka, a Computational Modeling & Data Analytics graduate from Virginia Tech with a passion for using data to understand complex problems and help people make better decisions. This portfolio is a reflection of my journey through research, analytics, machine learning, and business intelligence, showcasing projects that span healthcare, artificial intelligence, data visualization, and business analytics. Whether you're here to explore my work, learn about my experiences, or simply get to know me, I hope this portfolio gives you a sense of how I approach problems and the impact I strive to create.

Throughout my academic and professional experiences, I've had the opportunity to work on a wide variety of analytical challenges—from biomedical research and natural language processing to dashboard development and predictive modeling. These experiences have strengthened both my technical foundation and my ability to communicate complex findings in a way that supports real-world decision-making. I enjoy working with tools such as Python, SQL, R, Tableau, Power BI, and cloud technologies to transform raw data into meaningful insights and practical solutions.

What excites me most about analytics is its versatility. I enjoy exploring different industries and discovering how the same analytical thinking can solve completely different problems. Whether it's understanding patient outcomes, improving business processes, building machine learning models, or exploring financial trends, I'm motivated by the opportunity to ask meaningful questions, uncover patterns, and translate data into actionable recommendations. I believe that great analytics isn't just about finding answers—it's about asking the right questions, communicating insights clearly, and creating solutions that people can trust and use.

Outside of analytics, I enjoy taking on new challenges, continuously learning, and stepping outside my comfort zone. I believe curiosity is one of the most valuable skills anyone can have, and I try to bring that mindset into every project I work on. As I continue growing as an analytics professional, I hope to contribute to projects that create meaningful impact while continuing to learn from the people and industries around me.

What I like working on

Dashboards, KPI reporting, survey analysis, financial forecasting, public-health analytics, and research projects where data has to be cleaned, modeled, explained, and turned into a practical recommendation.

How I work

I care about clear problem framing, reproducible analysis, thoughtful visuals, and communicating insights in a way that technical and non-technical stakeholders can both use.
